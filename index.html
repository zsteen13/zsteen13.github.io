Service Work

Serving my community has always been a passion of mine. Here are some of the ways I have found to serve others.

MacTeach

When I was in high school, I tutored at 7AM before school at Oak Grove Elementary School in San Antonio, Texas. This program focused on mentoring and tutoring at-risk students. I was able to be present in the lives of children that did not have stable home lives and improved their reading, writing, and math skills by running a computer lab. I also tutored my peers during my lunch hour. Read this awesome news article written about MacTeach while I was a member!

The Big Event

Each year at Texas A&M University, I participate in the Big Event, an annual service project in which students volunteer around the Bryan-College Station area. In 2019 alone, over 2,000 residents were served during the Big Event! I've served as a volunteer providing labor and as a site leader providing administration, and am excited to participate again. Learn more here.

Aggies Beyond Campus

MacTeach helped me to develop a love for tutoring and mentoring, which led to the creation of ABC. ABC seeks to implement the tutoring and mentoring model for at-risk children around Texas A&M. Last year, a few other tutors and I went to College Hills Elementary School weekly to provide assistance to teachers in the classroom, and additionally provide individual help the lagging students.
